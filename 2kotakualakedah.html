About the Place

Muzium Kota Kuala Kedah is located within Kota Kuala Kedah, a historic fortress built to defend the Kedah Sultanate from foreign invasions. The museum showcases the history of the fort and its importance as a military stronghold.




Historical Background

Construction of the fort began in the eighteenth century under Sultan Muhammad Jiwa Zainal Adilin II. It was later occupied by the Bugis, Siamese, and British during different periods, making it one of Kedah's most significant historical landmarks.

Historical Significance

  • One of the oldest surviving forts in Malaysia.
  • Demonstrates Kedah's military history.
  • Preserves original cannons, gateways, and defensive walls.
  • Highlights maritime trade and colonial conflicts.

Visitor Information

Visitors can explore the historic fortress walls, entrance gate, cannons, and museum galleries that showcase the history of Kota Kuala Kedah. Information panels explain the fort's role in defending the Kedah Sultanate, while the surrounding area offers scenic views of the Kedah River and nearby fishing village.
