About the Place

Located in Merbok, Muzium Arkeologi Lembah Bujang preserves archaeological discoveries from the Bujang Valley, one of Southeast Asia's oldest civilizations.




Historical Background

The museum houses artefacts dating from the fourth to fourteenth centuries, including pottery, religious sculptures, inscriptions, and ancient temple remains that demonstrate early trade between the Malay Peninsula, India, and China.

Historical Significance

  • Malaysia's oldest archaeological museum.
  • Displays Hindu-Buddhist artefacts.
  • Preserves evidence of early international trade.
  • Supports archaeological research and education.

Visitor Information

Visitors can tour the original house, view historical artefacts, photographs, personal belongings, and interactive displays explaining Tun Dr. Mahathir's early life and achievements.
